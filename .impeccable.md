# GreenFN Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Solo financial advisors (FAs) working alone on desktop, managing leads, clients, tasks, and interactions throughout their workday. Users range from tech-savvy to non-technical — simplicity and clarity must never be sacrificed for sophistication. The tool should feel like a reliable colleague: always there, always clear, never in the way. The FA opens this app to start their day and returns to it constantly; it competes with spreadsheets and WhatsApp for their attention.

### Brand Personality
**Three words**: Intuitive, Professional, Decisive

GreenFN should feel like a well-designed wealth management firm's internal tool — not a startup, not an enterprise monster. It helps FAs make confident decisions quickly. Every screen should communicate: *you're in control, here's what matters, here's what to do next.*

Emotional goals: calm confidence, clarity without clutter, trust in the product.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Theme**: Light mode — FAs use this during business hours in offices.

**Color**: Signature professional green as the brand anchor. Not a bright lime or garish emerald — a mid-tone authoritative green that reads as "growth" and "trust" without shouting. Neutral surfaces should carry a faint green tint for subconscious brand cohesion.

**Reference feel**: Pipedrive's design ethos — clean, direct, purposeful. HubSpot/Zoho's structural clarity for CRM workflows. The app should feel like a premium tool, not enterprise bloatware.

**Anti-references**: Salesforce (too much happening, dashboard overwhelm), GoHighLevel (clunky, too many buttons, visual noise). No sharp aggressive fonts, no cramped dense layouts, no unnecessary decorative elements.

**Typography**: Pair a confident geometric sans-serif for headings with a warm humanist sans for body copy. Approachable but professional. Rounded forms over sharp angular ones.

**Spacing**: Generous breathing room. Information in digestible chunks. Not sparse, but never crowded.

**Interactions**: Simple, direct. Optimistic UI everywhere. Animations should be subtle and purposeful — transitions that confirm "yes, that worked" without being performative.

### Design Principles
1. **One thing at a time.** Each screen has one primary action. Secondary actions recede until needed. No decision fatigue.
2. **Information earns its place.** If a label, number, or element doesn't help the FA make a decision or take action, cut it.
3. **Green means go.** Brand green is reserved for primary actions and positive states (won deals, completed tasks). It reinforces decisive action.
4. **Calm by default.** Default states are quiet. Urgency is communicated precisely — only when something genuinely demands attention.
5. **Desktop-native density.** More information per screen than a mobile-first tool, but laid out with enough space that nothing feels crowded. FAs work on large monitors; use the space well.

### Typography Choices
- **Headings/Display**: Sora — geometric, confident, slightly rounded, not in the AI monoculture
- **Body/UI**: Figtree — humanist, warm, highly readable at small sizes, professional without being cold

### Color Palette (OKLCH)
- **Brand green**: `oklch(0.50 0.14 145)` — authoritative mid-green, trustworthy
- **Green hover/active**: `oklch(0.44 0.15 145)` — deeper for interaction feedback
- **Green light (backgrounds)**: `oklch(0.95 0.025 145)` — soft tinted surface
- **Green faint tint**: `oklch(0.98 0.008 145)` — barely-there page background tint
- **Neutral text**: `oklch(0.18 0.005 145)` — near-black with green undertone
- **Muted text**: `oklch(0.50 0.008 145)` — secondary information
- **Border**: `oklch(0.88 0.01 145)` — subtle, tinted dividers
- **Surface**: `oklch(0.995 0.004 145)` — off-white with green undertone
- **Destructive**: `oklch(0.55 0.20 25)` — clear red for warnings/overdue
- **Amber (due today)**: `oklch(0.72 0.15 75)` — warm amber, not aggressive
