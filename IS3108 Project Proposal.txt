Big Idea
An AI-powered second brain for financial advisors that keeps every lead, client, interaction, and next step organised in one place – so advisors always know who to follow up with, what was last discussed, and what needs to happen next.
In practice, many financial advisors still rely on fragmented and outdated methods such as spreadsheets, notebooks, or manually scrolling through messaging apps to track their clients and leads. Yet advisors operate as solo entrepreneurs, where organisation and consistent follow-ups directly affect earning outcomes. The lack of a structured system creates unnecessary friction and increases the risk of missed actions or forgotten conversations.
Our project addresses that problem by providing a single, structured workspace that mirrors an advisor’s ideal workflow. Clients and leads move through a visual pipeline that reflects their progress over time. Interactions with each client/lead are logged in a chronological history, to help advisors quickly recall prior discussions without rereading/recalling long or scattered conversations. Each client is also tied to clear next steps with set due dates, functioning as a to-do item that is scheduled in the near future or far ahead. These next steps collectively form a dynamic “Today” view that automatically surfaces overdue and upcoming actions, removing guesswork from daily task planning. By making client and lead management more efficient and frictionless, the system reduces administrative effort and allows advisors to spend more time on higher-value activities such as meeting clients and closing sales.

A central list of everyone the FA is handling, including new leads, warm prospects, and existing clients.


Why?
The FA finally has one clean, organised interface for all leads and clients instead of juggling multiple platforms such as WhatsApp, Telegram, Instagram etc from memory.


What?
* [DATA] Client key info:
   * Name
   * Contact details (phone, email)
   * Whether they are a Lead or Client
   * Where they came from (IG, referral, roadshow, etc.)
   * Birthday (as additional next action suggestion)
   * existing portfolio/policies

      * Input existing portfolios by filling up a form with relevant details and manually build out a client’s entire portfolio
         * Extension: Collate all policy information and generate into a policy summary PDF for FA to easily disseminate to clients

   * current priorities/life goals for touchpoint
      * Eg getting a BTO by XXX, marry by XXX, retire by XXX
         * manual input, can extend to AI scraping/searching for the info from long conversation transcripts/from generated summaries


* [ACTIONS] Allow simple search and filtering:
   * Find a specific person quickly
   * filter only leads / only clients
   * filter people from a specific source
   * allow for creation of tags to group different contacts together

Leads Pipeline [Moveable Cards]
Description:
A visual leads board that shows where each person is in the FA’s pipeline.
Why?
The FA can see their business at a glance. From the journey, we can see who is stuck, who is moving, and where attention is needed. 
What?
* [VISUALS] Show the journey as clear columns/statuses (for example):
   * New → Contacted → Booked → No-show → In Progress → Closed (Won) / Closed (Lost)
* Each person appears as a card in the appropriate column.
* [ACTIONS] The FA can drag and drop leads between statuses as leads progress.
To Do List [Urgent Action]
Description:
A simple way to decide and remember what needs to happen next for each person/lead, and a daily list that shows who to focus on.
Why?
Every person has a clear “next move”. The FA always knows who to work on today based on urgency, instead of relying on memory, random scrolling, or scattered notes.
What?
* [VISUAL] There is a “Today” view that shows:
   * Everyone whose next step is due today.
   * Everyone whose next step is overdue.


* [ACTION] For each person, the FA can set:
   * A short next step (e.g. “Send follow-up message”, “Confirm appointment”, “Check in after payday”, “Wish happy birthday”).
   * A date for that next step.
* Optional light nudge:
   * After moving a lead on the pipeline, the app can remind the FA to set a next step OR to update interaction history (see feature 4).
Additional Suggestions:


Assisted Messaging for Next Steps[c]
* When a message-related task is due, use AI to generate a message draft using contact, pipeline stage, and recent interaction data
* Users can review and edit the draft before sending
* GreenFN then opens the relevant external messaging platform (e.g. WhatsApp, Telegram) with the message pre-filled or ready to paste, user will then make the final send
* Also allow for users to create/modify their own reusable message templates for common scenarios (e.g. follow-ups, reminders, post-meeting messages), with simple placeholders that are automatically filled based on context and can optionally be refined using AI.
* This approach improves execution speed while deliberately avoiding full message automation and its associated legal, privacy, and platform-policy risks.


Conversation State Tracking (Response Accountability)[d]
* Outbound messages are treated as open communication loops rather than one-off actions.
* Each conversation can exist in one of four explicit states:
   * Awaiting response
   * Conversation in progress
   * Response received (action required)
   * Closed / no response expected
* Use these states to systematically track follow-up obligations without importing chat history or detecting inbound messages
* Unresolved conversation loops are surfaced alongside other due tasks/next steps in the Today view, ensuring accountability while relying on external messaging platforms for real-time notifications.


Interaction History [Personal, compared to Leads Pipeline feature]
Description:
A simple timeline for each person, showing what has happened with them over time.
Why?
Before contacting someone again, the FA can quickly scroll their history and remember what was shared, what was promised, and what’s important to that person.
What?
* [DATA] Each log entry has:
   * Interaction type
   * Date
   * Free-form notes (or AI summary, see next feature)


* [ACTIONS] For each person, the FA can log interactions such as:
   * Calls
   * Zoom/face-to-face meetings
   * WhatsApp/DM conversations
   * General notes
AI Interaction History/Summaries [Personal]
Description:
An AI-powered helper that turns each interaction into a concise but detailed summary, based on a short guided questionnaire.
Why?
Before the next call, the FA can skim a few summaries and instantly remember the full picture. Reduces mental load and makes every follow-up feel more personal and professional.
What?
* [ACTION] After a call/meeting/chat, instead of writing a long messy note, the FA:
   * Fills in a short, structured form about the interaction, e.g.:
      * What was the purpose of this interaction?
      * What did the client share about their situation?
      * What needs or problems came up?
      * What options/solutions were discussed?
      * What was decided (if anything)?
      * What are the next steps?
Alternative: AI-Assisted Conversation Summaries[e]
* After a conversation or appointment is completed, GreenFN prompts users to optionally capture key takeaways as part of the natural post-conversation workflow.
* Users may either:
   * paste existing AI-generated summaries from online meetings (e.g. Zoom, Google Meet), or
   * provide brief, unstructured notes for conversations without recordings, or
   * Copy-paste conversation history/messages from messaging apps
* AI is used to compress and standardise the input into a concise interaction summary that preserves key context for future recall.
* The prompt is lightweight and fully skippable, ensuring minimal disruption while supporting long-term memory and better context for subsequent actions.
* The app uses AI to turn those answers into a clean, concise written summary that:
   * Describes who this person is,
   * Highlights their key goals, concerns and context,
   * Recaps what was discussed,
   * Restates any decisions and agreed next steps.
